## What is Password Strength?

Password strength describes how resistant a password is to guessing and cracking attacks.

- Longer passphrases increase entropy and are easier to remember.
- Mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols improves variety.
- Avoid reused, common, or leaked passwords.

A short password like `123456` or `qwerty` is often cracked in less than a second. A longer unique passphrase like `Blue-Lemon!River-2026` can push estimated crack times to years or longer.

> Use a password manager to generate and store unique passwords.
