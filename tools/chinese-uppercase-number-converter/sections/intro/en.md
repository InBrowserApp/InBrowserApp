## What are Chinese uppercase numbers?

Chinese uppercase amounts are the formal numerals used on invoices,
receipts, and bank documents to reduce tampering. They replace ordinary
digits with financial characters such as 壹, 贰, 叁, 拾, 佰, and 仟.

This converter works in both directions: it can turn a plain number into
an RMB-style uppercase amount, or parse an uppercase amount back into a
number. It supports negative values, fixed 元/角/分 units, and up to two
decimal places.

### Common formatting rules

A full amount usually contains an integer part, the 元 unit, and an
optional fractional part written with 角 and 分. Whole amounts often end
with 整, while zeroes are inserted only where they are needed to keep the
wording clear.

For example, `1024.50` can be rendered as `壹仟零贰拾肆元伍角` in
Simplified output, or `壹仟零貳拾肆元伍角` in Traditional output.

### Simplified vs Traditional

Simplified output uses forms such as 贰, 叁, 陆, 万, and 亿. Traditional
output uses 貳, 參, 陸, 萬, and 億, while keeping the same financial
structure.

Choose the style that matches the document standard you need, then copy
the result directly into contracts, invoices, or accounting forms.
